My name was Kenji Takagi. I spent 14 years mastering Eternal Abyss, a game famed for its cruelty. Now I am Rio, a 5-year-old orphan in the slums of a fantasy world. I have no memories of how I died, only the cold, hard logic of the game’s systems burned into my brain. Below me, the city of Oakhaven is a maze of stone and misery. Above me, the high priest of the Church of light is preparing the purge. But they do not know that the system is active. And they do not know that I have already unlocked the void fox bloodline. The void fox bloodline is a forbidden class in Eternal Abyss, a class that doesn’t exist in the official manuals. I found it in the code of the beta server, a hidden variable that the developers forgot to delete. It gives you access to shadow steel, a material that cannot be cut by any blade or melted by any fire. It gives you access to the grimoire, a book of impossible power that can rewrite the rules of physical space. And it gives you access to the overload ritual, a desperate all-or-nothing gambit that converts your own blood into pure kinetic force. I have spent 3 years preparing Oakhaven slums. The slum dwellers are not my people yet, but they will be. I have built a fortress under the ruins of the old temple, using shadow steel bars I forged in the dark. I have trained 40 orphans in the art of silent movement and shadow daggers. I have stockpiled 2 tons of iron ore and 500 liters of acid. The high priest thinks he is coming to clean a nest of thieves. He is coming to a slaughterhouse. The high priest arrived at dawn. 40 Knights in silver armor, three templars with glowing swords, and 12 priests carrying light wells, devices that emit a blinding golden radiance that nullifies shadow magic. The slum dwellers ran. The guards closed the gates. Oakhaven was sealed. I stood on the roof of the old temple, a 5-year-old child in a dirty tunic, holding a book of black steel that hummed with a low vibration. The high priest saw me. He did not run. He did not call his knights. He smiled. It was the smile of a man who believed he was doing his gods work. He raised his hand, and the light wells began to hum. The light wells were the problem. In the game, they have a range of 50 m and a decay coefficient of 0.8. In this world, the light was thicker, heavier, it felt like warm oil on my skin. It was suppressing my void energy, keeping my mana pool from regenerating. I needed to destroy them first. I raised the grimoire. I activated the first spell: Shadow Link. A black line shot from my fingers, connecting the light wells to the iron ore under the temple. The light wells flickered. The golden light turned gray, then purple, then black. The priests carrying them screamed as the metal became hot, then melted their skin. The knights took a step back. I did not give them time to think. I activated the second spell: Shadow Steel Gale. 40 needles of black metal shot from the temple walls, moving at sound speed. They did not aim for the armor. They aimed for the eye slits. 12 knights fell in the first second. The templars screamed, their glowing swords cutting the air, but there was nothing to cut. The needles were gone, returning to the shadows behind them. The high priest’s smile vanished. He raised his staff, and a barrier of golden light appeared around him, deflecting the next wave of needles. He was a level 45 boss in the game. In this world, his level was hidden, but his mana output was identical. He was preparing the Light of judgement, a spell that would destroy the temple and everything inside it. I had 12 seconds. I looked at my mana pool: 120 units. The overload ritual required 300. I had only one source of mana left: my own blood. I bit my tongue. I let the blood run down my chin, and I spoke the words of the ritual. The system message appeared in my head: [OVERLOAD ACTIVE. MANA POOL EXTENDED TO 500. HEALTH DECAYING AT 2% PER SECOND.] The void energy returned, not slow, a flood of black fire that burned my veins. I raised both hands. I did not aim for the high priest. I aimed for the temple pillars. The black fire hit the stone, and the stone became dust. The roof collapsed. The high priest’s barrier held against the falling stone, but his knights were not so lucky. The temple fell on them, burying 20 knights and two templars under tons of granite. The remaining templar charged me, his glowing blade inches from my face. I did not move. I let him strike. The blade hit my chest, and the sound was like glass breaking. The blade did not go in. My skin had turned to shadow steel, the void fox bloodline's passive defense. I grabbed his wrist with my 5-year-old hand, and I twisted. The bone broke cleanly. I took his sword. The glowing blade turned black as my mana entered it. I used his own sword to cut his head off. The high priest was still standing under his barrier, his staff glowing with a blinding white light. The spell was complete. The Light of judgement came down from the sky, a beam of pure gold that hit me directly. It didn't burn. It was cold. It felt like my soul was being pulled out through my eyes. My health pool dropped: 80%, 60%, 40%, 20%. The system message was flashing red: [CRITICAL HEALTH. DESTRUCTIVE IMPACT DETECTED. CHOOSE RECOVERY TYPE.] I chose void rebirth. The red message turned black. My health pool froze at 1%. The golden beam shattered. The high priest stood there, his staff empty of light, his face white, his eyes wide. He looked at me, a 5-year-old child with black eyes, holding a black sword, standing in the middle of a ruined temple surrounded by the bodies of his knights. He turned to run. I did not use a spell. I walked up to him, and I pushed the black sword through his throat from behind. He fell on his knees. I took his staff. The golden gem at the top turned black in my hand. The system message appeared: [PURGE COMPLETED. 4500 EXPERIENCE POINTS ACQUIRED. FORBIDDEN CLASS LEVEL UP: 12. NEW SKILL ACQUIRED: VOID DOMAIN.] I stood in the middle of the ruins, my body cold, my hands shaking, my breath ragged. The slum dwellers were coming out of their hiding places, looking at the ruins and the bodies and the child who was holding the black staff. They did not shout. They did not cheer. They looked at me with the fear that people reserve for gods. I raised the staff. My little village had become a fortress. My people were safe, but I was no longer just fighting for their survival. I was fighting for the future of the entire world. The stakes had been raised to an unimaginable level, and the next move was mine.
